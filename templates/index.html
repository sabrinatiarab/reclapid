Specification Guide

Processor

What's That? 
The brains of the laptop, the better the processor, the faster your computer will run.
Recommendation:
For a dependable laptop, an Intel i3 is fine, but an i5 will guarantee good speeds. Laptops with i7 chips cost a lot more, and are more suited to those running design software or games.

Screen

What's That?
Size and resolution of screen will have a big impact on your experience.
Recommendation:
It's best not to go smaller than a 13-inch screen, though you can live without 4K displays unless you're a professional designer or photo-editor. Full HD resolution is fine.

Storage

What's That?
The amount of space you can use to store your files.
Recommendation:
It's best not to accept less than 256GB for a solid state drive (SSD, which helps laptops run faster), or less than 1TB for a traditional hard drive (not as fast, but more generous with the storage).

RAM

What's That?
Used for juggling multiple applications at once. More RAM can give you a speed boost.
Recommendation:
These days, 8GB RAM is the minimum to aim for. 16GB or 32GB is only needed for high-end machines.

Weight

What's That?
The weight of a laptop is what tends to most directly affect the portability of a computer. Look for the "travel weight" to get a more accurate estimate of real-world weight.
Recommendation:
Normally, weight is relevant to screen size. But some laptops with larger screens are becoming even lighter and more portable. The average laptop weight varies depending on screen size. Generally:
Small (14-inch) - 1.5 to 2kg
Medium (16-inch) - 2 to 2.5kg
Large (17-inch) - 3kg
